1 Simplified model

It is assumed that there are two sets of tasks, A and B, which are associated with aging-independent mortality rates mA and mB respectively. In the simplified model aging does not affect the mortality of workers until a certain age is reached, when resources become exhausted. At that time all workers die. A worker cannot spend more than the maximum resource k available for the whole life. The rates of resource expenditure during tasks A and B are cA and cB respectively. In the model the expected longevity of workers in colonies with and without caste polyethism is compared. If there is no polyethism, the workers perform tasks A and B in turn. If caste polyethism is present the workers from caste A perform only A-type tasks during the whole life and the workers from caste B perform only B-type tasks during the whole life.
